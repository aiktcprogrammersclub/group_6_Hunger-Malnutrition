Some More

This will be done through strategies of working with government systems, community mobilization and directly running child care facilities (20-30 crèches per block) in some select villages with specific vulnerability.

Specific Strategies:

  • Community mobilization and sharing of information through Participatory Learning Activity (PLA) (Ekjut model)

  • Creches/Day Care Centres (JSS model) – early stimulation; managing risk and malnourished children; regular growth monitoring.

  • System strengthening and policy advocacy (PHRN model) – functional Anganwadiscentres; functional mother’s committee; functional VHSC; functional VHND, referral services; convergence; community based monitoring; social audits; networking.

  • Research and Documentation for creating evidence base (Ekjut/PHRN)

The specific objectives are:

    PHRN feels that this small model will help to inform the state governments towards developing and modifying its own strategy. Since this is being done as action research, a full-fledged research protocol and MIS is being developed that will also provide helpful data in a situation where there are large time gaps between national and state surveys. During implementing this project; we intend to respect state government protocols for MTCs/NRCs and related efforts, and not duplicate state government services. We believe this programme will complement all the efforts that the government is making in Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand and Odisha on malnutrition and will lead to model-building and greater operational understanding.
